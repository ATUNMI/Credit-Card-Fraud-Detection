📌 Overview

In modern fintech ecosystems, fraud is no longer a passive cost—it is an adaptive, evolving threat. This project delivers a data-driven analysis of credit card fraud, designed to enhance detection capabilities and enable proactive risk mitigation.

The solution is built as a decision-first intelligence dashboard for risk managers and fraud analysts, providing visibility into transaction risk patterns, geographic hotspots, and temporal fraud trends.

🎯 Objectives

Identify high-risk transaction patterns and fraud vectors

Enable rapid prioritization of fraud investigations

Provide actionable insights for risk mitigation strategies

Support data-driven decision-making in fraud operations

📊 Key Risk Indicators (KRIs)

| Metric                      | Value   | Description                                               |
|---------------------------|--------|-----------------------------------------------------------|
| Fraud Rate                | 28.60% | Percentage of transactions classified as fraudulent        |
| Fraudulent Transactions   | 286    | Total confirmed fraud cases                               |
| Total Fraud Exposure      | 3M     | Aggregate financial loss from fraud                        |
| Critical Risk Transactions | 10.70% | High-severity cases requiring immediate action            |
| Primary Fraud Vector      | Card-Not-Present | Most frequent fraud channel                      |

🔍 Features & Analysis
1. Fraud Type & Merchant Segmentation

Breaks down transaction values across fraud types and merchant categories

Enables targeted controls at the merchant/industry level

2. Risk Tier Distribution

Transactions are classified into four tiers:

Low: 42.4%

Medium: 27.9%

High: 18.8%

Critical: 10.9%

➡️ Supports efficient allocation of investigative resources

3. Geographic Risk Mapping

Identifies fraud concentration by region

Highlights high-incidence areas (e.g., Maharashtra, Karnataka)

Enables localized fraud control strategies

4. Temporal Trend Analysis

Tracks fraud activity across months

Detects seasonality patterns

Identifies December spike, indicating holiday-driven fraud risk

💡 Key Insights

Card-Not-Present (CNP) transactions represent the dominant fraud vector

Fraud activity peaks during Q4, with a significant surge in December

A meaningful share of transactions falls within High and Critical risk tiers, requiring robust triage mechanisms

✅ Recommendations
Strengthen Digital Transaction Controls

Implement 3D Secure authentication

Introduce multi-factor authentication (MFA) for online payments

Adopt Seasonal Risk Strategies

Increase monitoring sensitivity during Q4

Scale fraud operations capacity during peak periods

Enable Targeted Risk Mitigation

Apply granular controls using merchant-level segmentation

Deploy region-specific fraud prevention strategies
